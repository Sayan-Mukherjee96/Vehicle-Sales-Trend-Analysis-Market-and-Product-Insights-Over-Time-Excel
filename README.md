📊Vehicle Sales Trend Analysis: Market and Product Insights Over Time
“The market and product trend analysis of BMW sales reveals that regional performance, engine preferences, and pricing are the key factors influencing revenue and demand.”

⭐Overview
This project provides an overview of BMW sales performance by analyzing trends across regions, product categories, pricing, and years.

🔍Problem Statement
BMW faces challenges in understanding how regional performance, product characteristics, and pricing strategies influence overall sales and revenue, limiting its ability to identify key business drivers and make data-driven decisions.

📁 Dataset
The dataset contains BMW vehicle sales data across different regions and years, including the following variables:
Model
Engine Size
Fuel Type
Transmission
Price
Units Sold

🛠 Tools Used
Microsoft Excel
Pivot Tables
Charts
Data Visualization

💡Key Insights
1. The United States is the strongest market, leading in both revenue and unit sales compared to Asia and Europe.

2. Sales and revenue peaked in 2019, declined sharply in 2020, and gradually recovered afterward, highlighting market volatility over time.

3. Mid-sized engines (2.5L) have the highest demand, indicating that customers prefer a balance between performance and fuel efficiency.

4. Electric vehicles dominate the high-sales segment, while hybrids lead the medium category, indicating a shift toward alternative fuel adoption.

5. Automatic transmissions dominate most years, with occasional spikes in manual transmission demand.

6. The 3 Series records the highest unit sales despite a lower price point, while premium models sell fewer units at higher prices.

7. Pricing alone does not drive demand, as several lower-priced models outperform premium models in sales volume.

📈 Results
The United States leads in both revenue and units sold, with peak performance in 2019 followed by fluctuations in subsequent years. Mid-sized engines account for the highest sales, while electric and hybrid vehicles show increasing presence. Additionally, lower-priced models tend to achieve higher sales volumes compared to premium models.

🧠 Conclusion
Overall, the analysis shows that customer preferences, regional demand, and product positioning are key drivers of sales performance. Markets with strong demand, balanced product offerings, and optimized pricing strategies achieve better results, highlighting the importance of data-driven decision-making for business growth.
